Rating Pathway

S1 through C3

OBS
Observer
STARTING POINT
All new VATSIM members start as Observer. You can observe ATC operations on any frequency but cannot control. Complete the VATSIM New Member Orientation Course.
S1
Developing Controller
VATSIM S1
First ATC rating. Authorised to control Delivery (DEL) and Ground (GND) positions. Covers radio telephony, ground movement, pushback and clearance delivery.
Positions: DEL, GND at authorised airports
S2
Aerodrome Controller
VATSIM S2
Tower control added. Manages aircraft in the circuit, on final approach, and on the runway. Traffic sequencing, wake turbulence separation, runway crossings.
Positions: TWR + all S1 positions
S3
Terminal Controller
VATSIM S3
Approach and Departure control. Manages IFR traffic in the terminal area, radar vectoring, holding, and sequencing arrivals and departures.
Positions: APP, DEP + all S2 positions
C1
Enroute Controller
VATSIM C1
Full enroute control. Manages aircraft in controlled airspace above the terminal area. Oceanic procedures available with endorsement. All ATC facilities unlocked.
Positions: CTR + all S3 positions
C3
Senior Controller
VATSIM C3 — AWARDED BY DIVISION
Awarded by VATPAC for seniority and distinguished service. Functionally equivalent to C1 — same positions. A mark of recognition, not additional authority.
All C1 positions + recognition status

ATC Training Curriculum

ATC-01Foundation — Towards S1
+
ℹ
Recommended prerequisite: at least 20 hours as a pilot on VATSIM so you understand the pilot perspective before controlling.
Module 1 — ATC Fundamentals
Role of ATC — separation, flow management, information
Australian airspace classification — Class A, C, D, E, G
VHF communication — frequencies, transmission technique
ICAO phonetic alphabet and standard phraseology
ATIS construction and broadcast
Module 2 — Ground Operations (S1)
Clearance Delivery — IFR clearance format (CRAFT)
SID assignment — departure procedures and initial altitudes
Squawk code assignment and transponder instructions
Ground movement control — taxi routes, hold short instructions
Runway crossings — coordination with Tower
Pushback authorisation and coordination
Module 3 — Practical S1 Sessions (x4)
Session 1: SweatBox simulation — DEL at Sydney (YSSY)
Session 2: SweatBox — GND at Melbourne (YMML)
Session 3: Supervised live VATSIM session with QFV mentor
Session 4: S1 readiness assessment with QFV instructor
ATC-02Aerodrome Control — Towards S2
+
Module 1 — Tower Operations
Runway management — landing clearances, line-up and wait
VFR circuit management — sequencing, extend downwind
Wake turbulence separation — categories and minima
Special VFR operations in controlled airspace
Low visibility operations — CAT II/III procedures
Module 2 — Traffic Management
Sequencing multiple arrivals — speed control, extending paths
Coordination with Approach and Ground
Go-around handling — instructions and sequencing
Emergencies — declaring Mayday, emergency services notification
Module 3 — Practice Sessions (x4)
Sessions 1-2: SweatBox TWR at YSSY and YMML
Session 3: Supervised live VATSIM TWR with QFV instructor
Session 4: S2 readiness assessment
ATC-03Terminal Control — Towards S3
+
Module 1 — Radar Fundamentals
Radar identification — surveillance radar, SSR, primary
Radar separation standards — longitudinal and lateral
Vectoring for approach — intercept angles, speed control
Missed approach handling — radar identification, re-sequencing
ILS critical area protection
Module 2 — Approach Sequencing
STAR management — speed restrictions, altitude crossing
Sequence building — determining order, managing flow
Holding — assigning, managing and dissolving holds
Parallel runway operations — YSSY 34L/R
Adjacent sector coordination
ATC-04Enroute Control — Towards C1
+
Module 1 — Enroute Operations
ATS route structure in Australian airspace
Conflict detection — lateral, vertical, longitudinal
RVSM airspace — 1,000ft separation above FL290
Re-routing, weather deviations, off-track clearances
Coordination between sectors — boundary, transfer of control
Module 2 — Oceanic Control
Oceanic clearances — AUSOTS track allocation
Mach number technique — longitudinal oceanic separation
CPDLC — Controller Pilot Data Link Communications
ADS-C — automatic dependent surveillance contract
HF voice position reports and oceanic re-clearances
