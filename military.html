Military Wing

About the Military Division

The QFV Military Division (QFV-MIL) operates alongside the civilian division, conducting special operations, tactical transport, and military exercise events on VATSIM. We fly real RAAF-style missions including troop transport, maritime patrol simulation, strategic airlift, and special forces insertion exercises.

Military ratings M1–M4 mirror the official VATSIM Military Rating system. All military flights must still use SimBrief for planning and Flight Logger for logging.

⚠
SimBrief & Flight Logger are still mandatory for all military flights. Use aircraft type MRTT, C17, PC21, or applicable type in SimBrief.

Military Ratings

M1 — M4 Progression

M0
No Military Rating
DEFAULT — ALL MEMBERS
Standard civilian member. No access to military operations or channels.
M1
Military Pilot Licence
ENTRY — BASIC MILITARY
Completed Military Induction Programme. 10+ military flight hours. Access to transport missions and basic exercises.
Unlocks: C-130 transport, basic patrol, exercise participation
M2
Military Instrument Rating
INTERMEDIATE
M1 + IFR military procedures + 25 total military hours. All-weather and night operation capabilities.
Unlocks: Night ops, all-weather missions, maritime patrol
M3
Military Multi-Engine Rating
ADVANCED
M2 + multi-engine military operations + 50 total military hours. Heavy transport and tanker operations.
Unlocks: C-17 Globemaster, KC-30A tanker, heavy airlift ops
M4
Military Mission Ready Pilot
ELITE — FULL MILITARY AUTHORITY
M3 + 100 total military hours + staff endorsement. Highest military rating. Mission lead authority on all exercise types.
Unlocks: Special Ops, mission lead authority, classified exercises

Military Training Curriculum

Structured training programme for military division entry and progression. Complements civilian pilot training.

+

Module 1 — Military Operations Overview
RAAF structure and how QFV-MIL models RAAF operations
Rules of Engagement (ROE) within QFV simulation exercises
Military callsign format and radio phraseology differences
Mission planning — objectives, routes, fuel, contingencies
Military brevity codes used in QFV exercises

Supervised C-130 tactical transport mission with M4 instructor
Formation flying basics — position keeping, communication
M1 assessment flight — mission execution and debrief

+
