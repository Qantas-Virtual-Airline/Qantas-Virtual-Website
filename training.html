Programme Overview

How Training Works

ℹ
About QFV Training: QFV provides structured training materials, mentorship, and supervised flights to prepare you for official VATSIM ratings. All theory and practice is done within QFV. Official ratings are issued by VATSIM ATOs. When QFV reaches VAP status, we will apply to become a VATSIM ATO.
⚠
Mandatory Tools: Every training flight must be planned via SimBrief and logged via Flight Logger. Training flights count toward your hours only when both tools are used correctly.
✓
All experience levels welcome. Start at Phase 1 regardless of your simulator experience. Progress at your own pace with support from QFV mentors.

PHASE 1Private Pilot Licence — P1 (PPL)
+
ℹ
Target aircraft: B737-800. Covers VFR fundamentals, basic airmanship, and first solo flights on VATSIM. Prepares for VATSIM P1 PPL(A) examination at an authorised ATO.
Module 1 — Theory of Flight
Four forces: lift, weight, drag, thrust
Aerofoil design and how wings generate lift
Primary and secondary flight controls — operation and effect
Aircraft stability: longitudinal, lateral, directional
Stalls, spins, and recovery procedures
Module 2 — Meteorology
Reading METAR and TAF reports
Cloud types and their significance to VFR flight
VFR weather minima — visibility and cloud clearance
Wind: surface, gradient, and their effects on flight
Thunderstorms, turbulence, and icing awareness
Module 3 — Navigation
Aeronautical charts — reading WAC, VNC, and TAC charts
Dead reckoning navigation fundamentals
Magnetic variation and compass errors
VOR, NDB, and GPS basics for VFR navigation
Airspace structure in Australia — Class A, C, D, E, G
Module 4 — Radio Communications (VFR)
ICAO phonetic alphabet and standard phraseology
Communication with Ground, Tower, Approach on VATSIM
Readbacks, acknowledgements, and corrections
Handling loss of communications (NORDO)
Practical: first solo radio communications session on VATSIM
Module 5 — Practical Flights (x6 supervised)
Flight 1: Straight & level, basic turns, climbs and descents
Flight 2: Circuit training — take-off, circuit pattern, landing
Flight 3: Navigation exercise — local area VFR flight
Flight 4: Emergency procedures — engine failure simulation
Flight 5: Cross-country VFR navigation with ATC
Flight 6: Skills test preparation flight with QFV mentor

Mentorship

QFV Mentor Programme

QFV mentors are experienced pilots (P4+ rated) who have completed mentor training. They conduct supervised training flights, provide feedback, and help students prepare for VATSIM ATO examinations.

✓
Applying to be a Mentor: You must hold P4 (ATPL) rating, have 50+ logged QFV hours, and be recommended by the Director of Pilot Training. Apply via the support page or Discord.
MENTOR RESPONSIBILITIES
Conduct supervised training flights with students
Provide written debrief after every session
Mark student progress in the training system
Recommend students for ATO examination when ready
Minimum 2 mentoring sessions per month to retain role
