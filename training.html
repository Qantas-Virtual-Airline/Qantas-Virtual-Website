How Training Works

ℹ
About QFV Training: QFV provides structured materials, mentorship, and supervised flights to prepare you for official VATSIM ratings. Official ratings are issued by VATSIM ATOs, not QFV. When QFV reaches VAP status, we will apply to become a VATSIM ATO.
⚠
Mandatory: Every training flight requires SimBrief OFP + Flight Logger submission.
PHASE 0New Member Orientation — P0
+
Module 1 — VATSIM Fundamentals
Complete the VATSIM New Member Orientation Course at vatsim.net
VATSIM Code of Conduct — read and understand all rules
Create your VATSIM account and note your CID
Set up SimBrief account and generate a practice OFP
Install Flight Logger bot in QFV Discord and submit a test entry
Module 2 — Simulator Setup
Install and configure a VATSIM pilot client (vPilot / xPilot / Swift)
Connect to VATSIM for the first time — step by step with a mentor
Understand squawk codes and transponder modes A/C vs S
PHASE 1Private Pilot Licence — P1 (PPL)
+
✓
Target aircraft: B737-800. Covers VFR fundamentals, basic airmanship, first solo flights on VATSIM.
Module 1 — Theory of Flight
Four forces: lift, weight, drag, thrust
Primary and secondary flight controls
Stalls, spins, and recovery procedures
Aircraft stability — longitudinal, lateral, directional
Module 2 — Meteorology
Reading METAR and TAF reports
VFR weather minima — visibility and cloud clearance
Wind effects on flight — surface, gradient, crosswind
Module 3 — Radio Communications (VFR)
ICAO phonetic alphabet and standard phraseology
Communicating with Ground, Tower, Approach on VATSIM
Readbacks, acknowledgements, and corrections
Module 4 — Practical Flights (×6 supervised)
Flight 1: Straight & level, basic turns, climbs and descents
Flight 2: Circuit training — takeoff, pattern, landing
Flight 3: Navigation exercise — local area VFR
Flight 4: Emergency procedures — engine failure simulation
Flight 5: Cross-country VFR with ATC
Flight 6: P1 skills test preparation with QFV mentor
PHASE 2Instrument Rating — P2 (IR)
+
ℹ
Target aircraft: B737-800, A321XLR. Full IFR operations, SID/STAR procedures, instrument approaches.
Module 1 — IFR Fundamentals
Instrument scanning and cross-checking technique
Attitude flying by instruments — pitch, bank, power
Unusual attitude recovery under instruments
Module 2 — IFR Navigation
Filing IFR flight plans on VATSIM
SID and STAR procedures — reading and flying
Holding patterns — entry, timing, corrections
Module 3 — Instrument Approaches
ILS approach — full procedure from initial to touchdown
RNAV/GNSS approaches — LPV, LNAV/VNAV
Approach minima — DA, MDA, and go-around decision
Module 4 — SimBrief IFR Planning
Generating full IFR OFP in SimBrief for B738
Fuel planning: block, trip, contingency, alternate, final reserve
Loading SimBrief OFP into FMS via integration
PHASE 3Commercial Multi-Engine — P3 (CMEL)
+
ℹ
Target aircraft: A330-200/300. Multi-engine ops, ETOPS, oceanic procedures. Unlocks international routes.
Module 1 — Multi-Engine Theory
Multi-engine aerodynamics — yaw, critical engine, Vmc
Engine failure recognition and immediate actions
Airbus fly-by-wire fundamentals — normal, alternate, direct law
Module 2 — International Operations
ETOPS-180 — what it means, planning requirements
Oceanic procedures — SELCAL, HF, CPDLC, position reports
Pacific and Indian Ocean track systems
PHASE 4Airline Transport Pilot — P4 (ATPL)
+
ℹ
Target aircraft: B787-9. Highest operational rating. Advanced airmanship, complex systems, ultra-long-haul.
Module 1 — Advanced Airmanship
Threat & Error Management (TEM)
Crew Resource Management (CRM)
Stabilised approach criteria and go-around decision
Module 2 — Ultra-Long-Haul Operations
Fuel planning for 15+ hour flights — PER–LHR methodology
Track systems — AUSOTS, PACOTS, NAT
SIGMET interpretation — turbulence, CB avoidance
PHASE 5A380 Endorsement + FI/FE Ratings
+
★
Prerequisite: P4 (ATPL) required. A380 endorsement is the most prestigious qualification within QFV.
A380 Type Endorsement
A380 systems differences from A330 — 4 engines, 8 hydraulics, trim tank
SUPER wake turbulence — VATSIM filing A388/H, separation
Airport compatibility — Code F infrastructure required
Supervised check flight: SYD–LHR with QFV examiner
Flight Instructor (P5) / Examiner (P6)
Instructor techniques — brief, debrief, mentor students
QFV Examiner Standards — grading criteria
Complete P5/P6 through approved VATSIM ATO
